## Issue Reported

## Internal Notes (Agent: Maria Lopez)

Received ticket from Tom Baker reporting printer showing offline status. Steps taken:

1. Confirmed which printer was affected -- HP LaserJet on floor 2
2. Checked that the printer was physically powered on and connected -- it was
3. Remotely accessed Tom's workstation to inspect the print queue
4. Found the print spooler service had stopped -- this was causing the offline status
5. Cleared 3 stuck jobs sitting in the print queue from previous users
6. Restarted the Print Spooler service via Services console
7. Printer came back online immediately after spooler restart
8. Uninstalled and reinstalled the printer driver to prevent recurrence
9. Sent a test print to confirm functionality -- successful

Root cause: The Windows Print Spooler service crashed due to a corrupted print job left in the queue by another user.
