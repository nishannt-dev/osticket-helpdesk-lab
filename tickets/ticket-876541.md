## Issue Reported

> "After moving to my new desk my second monitor is completely black. The computer detects it but nothing shows."

---

## Internal Notes (Agent: Nishant)

Received ticket from James Reed following an office desk move. Second monitor detected by Windows but displaying no image. Steps taken:

1. Visited James's desk in person to assess the setup
2. Confirmed Windows was detecting the second monitor in Display Settings -- showed as active but black
3. Checked all cable connections at both the monitor and the laptop -- appeared secure on visual inspection
4. Tested the DisplayPort cable by swapping it with a known working cable from IT spares
5. Second monitor immediately displayed correctly with the replacement cable
6. Inspected the original cable -- found a visible kink and partial internal break near the connector end, consistent with physical stress during the move
7. Updated display driver to latest version as a precautionary step
8. Reconfigured dual monitor layout in Display Settings to match James's preferred arrangement
9. Ran the setup for 10 minutes to confirm stability -- no flickering or dropout
10. Logged the damaged cable as disposed and noted the replacement in IT asset records

Root cause: The original DisplayPort cable was physically damaged during the desk relocation, causing signal loss to the second monitor despite Windows detecting its presence.

---

## Resolution Reply (Agent: Nishant)

> Hi James, I found the issue -- the DisplayPort cable connecting your second monitor was physically damaged during the desk move. I have replaced it with a new cable from our IT spares and your dual monitor setup is fully working again. The display layout has been configured to your preference. Let us know if anything shifts or if you notice any flickering.
